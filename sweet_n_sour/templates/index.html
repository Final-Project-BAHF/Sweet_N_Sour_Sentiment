Summary - Sentiment - Sweet and Sour:

This project seeks to analyze the Earnings Statements using sentiment analysis. Can automated sentiment analysis of a Company’s quarterly earnings call provide a glimpse of the short-term price movements to come during trading hours? For our final project, we will analyze the earnings call transcripts (text) of [100] of the largest companies in the United States, which are listed on a national stock exchange, indicated by their presence in the highly monitored “Standard and Poor 500 Index”. Specifically, we will look to analyze the sentiment within the following categories of the earnings call: i) management presentation, ii) Wall Street sell-side analyst questions, and iii) corresponding management answers to those questions.

Preliminary Hypothesis: A higher “sentiment score” rating across a Company’s earnings call transcript will indicate more positive stock price movement during the market trading day following the call. Conversely, lower “sentiment scores” across the earnings call transcript will indicate negative stock price movement during the market trading day following the call.

Null Hypothesis: There is no relationship between the CEO rambling and the next day activity of the realted stock.

In order to perform this analysis, we will train our own data using a list of over 3000 sentences then test against captured statements from S&P 500 earning statements
